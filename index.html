Step 3 — Gap analysis matrix
Gap = Required proficiency − Current proficiency. Colour indicates severity. Sort by function or competency.
View by:
Critical (gap ≥ 3) High (gap 2–3) Moderate (gap 1–2) Low (gap < 1)
Key findings from analysis
⚠
Strategic thinking is the widest gap
Average gap of 4.2 pts — consistent across all roles and functions. Root cause: hiring has prioritised technical delivery over business thinking.
⚠
Financial acumen gap is role-agnostic
Even Sales/BD managers score low. Suggests a systemic gap, not a role-specific one. Linked to difficulty building business cases for new pursuits.
△
Data skills gap is function-specific
Technology BU gap is 1.8 pts. Commercial/Sales BU gap is 3.4 pts — they have the least data exposure despite the most need for analytics in client conversations.
○
Coaching & people development — lower priority
Gap is real (2.1 pts) but less business-critical in FY26. Recommend including in FY27 planning cycle.
Root cause analysis
Why do these gaps exist?
OrgPromotion criteria focused on delivery metrics, not leadership capability
OrgNo structured development at M4–M5 level previously
RoleManagers rarely exposed to P&L or business case conversations
RoleLimited cross-functional rotation — siloed career paths
SkillSelf-assessed vs. 360° scores show consistent over-rating by 1.2 pts avg.
EnvNo on-the-job stretch assignments or action learning culture

Step 5 — Learning solution design
Match each training need to the most appropriate intervention. Consider cost, reach, depth, and timeline.
Intervention types — reference guide
MDPManagement Development Program — intensive multi-module residential or blended program. Best for deep behavioural change. 3–5 day, cohort-based.
WorkshopFocused 1–2 day intervention on a specific skill. Good for knowledge and tools. Quick to deploy.
CoachingIndividual or peer coaching over 3–6 months. Best for leadership mindset shifts and senior executives.
Action learningTeams solve real business challenges under facilitated guidance. High transfer, requires buy-in.
eLearningSelf-paced digital modules. High reach, low cost per head. Good for knowledge, poor for skill practice.
On-the-jobStretch assignments, job rotation, shadowing. Highest transfer — requires manager involvement.

Renewal signals — what to watch
✓
High NPS (≥70) + program ending in 90 days
Strongest renewal signal — send proposal within 2 weeks of program end
✓
Client sponsor shares results internally
When they present to their leadership, they are building internal buy-in for renewal
△
Asks about next cohort before current ends
Good signal — respond with a phased proposal that expands scope
△
New role group not covered in current program
Upsell opportunity — propose an extension for the next level
⚠
Budget freeze or leadership change at client
Pause outreach, re-engage after transition. Do not push — it reads as tone-deaf.
Upsell playbook
Expand to next cohort
Same program, next batch. Easiest renewal — reference existing results, propose dates immediately.
Expand to next level
If senior managers completed the MDP, propose a program for their direct reports. Natural cascade.
Add a new competency track
Use TNA data to propose a follow-on track addressing gaps not covered in the first program.
Enterprise-wide license
After 2+ successful programs, propose an annual retainer covering all L&D needs across BUs.
Refer to other BUs
Ask the sponsor to introduce you to L&D heads in other business units — warm referral is 5× faster than cold outreach.
