Step 3 — Gap analysis matrix
Gap = Required proficiency − Current proficiency. Colour indicates severity. Sort by function or competency.
View by:
Critical (gap ≥ 3) High (gap 2–3) Moderate (gap 1–2) Low (gap < 1)
Key findings from analysis
⚠
Strategic thinking is the widest gap
Average gap of 4.2 pts — consistent across all roles and functions. Root cause: hiring has prioritised technical delivery over business thinking.
⚠
Financial acumen gap is role-agnostic
Even Sales/BD managers score low. Suggests a systemic gap, not a role-specific one. Linked to difficulty building business cases for new pursuits.
△
Data skills gap is function-specific
Technology BU gap is 1.8 pts. Commercial/Sales BU gap is 3.4 pts — they have the least data exposure despite the most need for analytics in client conversations.
○
Coaching & people development — lower priority
Gap is real (2.1 pts) but less business-critical in FY26. Recommend including in FY27 planning cycle.
Root cause analysis
Why do these gaps exist?
OrgPromotion criteria focused on delivery metrics, not leadership capability
OrgNo structured development at M4–M5 level previously
RoleManagers rarely exposed to P&L or business case conversations
RoleLimited cross-functional rotation — siloed career paths
SkillSelf-assessed vs. 360° scores show consistent over-rating by 1.2 pts avg.
EnvNo on-the-job stretch assignments or action learning culture

Step 5 — Learning solution design
Match each training need to the most appropriate intervention. Consider cost, reach, depth, and timeline.
Intervention types — reference guide
MDPManagement Development Program — intensive multi-module residential or blended program. Best for deep behavioural change. 3–5 day, cohort-based.
WorkshopFocused 1–2 day intervention on a specific skill. Good for knowledge and tools. Quick to deploy.
CoachingIndividual or peer coaching over 3–6 months. Best for leadership mindset shifts and senior executives.
Action learningTeams solve real business challenges under facilitated guidance. High transfer, requires buy-in.
eLearningSelf-paced digital modules. High reach, low cost per head. Good for knowledge, poor for skill practice.
On-the-jobStretch assignments, job rotation, shadowing. Highest transfer — requires manager involvement.

TNA objectives
Participant self-assessment survey
This survey goes to all participants before the TNA. It establishes self-rated baseline proficiency and learning priorities.
Survey introduction (shown to participants)
Dear [Name], as part of our Training Needs Analysis, we would like to understand your current skill levels and learning priorities. This survey is confidential and will take approximately 15 minutes. Your honest responses will directly shape the development program designed for you.
Participant interview guide
Format: 45-minute 1-on-1 structured interview  ·  Who: Sample of 10–15% of participants (or all if group <20)  ·  Mode: In-person preferred, video call acceptable
Interviewer instructions
Begin with 5 minutes of rapport building — ask about their role history and current responsibilities before going into the guide. Use the STAR technique (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioural questions. Take notes using the template below — do NOT share notes with the participant's manager. Allow silences — give participants time to think before prompting. End by asking: "Is there anything else about your development you'd like me to know?"
Manager interview guide
Format: 30-minute structured interview  ·  Who: Direct managers / BU heads of all participant groups  ·  Mode: In-person or video — schedule at least 2 weeks in advance
Peer interview guide
Format: 20-minute structured conversation  ·  Who: 2–3 peers per participant (nominated by participant)  ·  Mode: Video call or in-person · confidential
360° TNA instrument
Each participant receives ratings from self, manager, 2–3 peers, and 1–2 direct reports on the same competency scale. This triangulates the self-assessment with observed behaviour.
360° administration notes
Send to raters 10 working days before the TNA synthesis session. Allow 7 days for completion. Send one reminder at Day 4. Ensure anonymity — pool peer and direct report ratings so individual responses cannot be identified. Share only aggregated scores with participants — never individual rater responses. The TNA lead reviews all 360 data before it is shared with the client.
Focus group facilitation guide
Format: 90-minute cross-functional group discussion  ·  Group size: 8–10 participants  ·  Recommended groups: 2–3 groups  ·  Mode: In-person strongly recommended
